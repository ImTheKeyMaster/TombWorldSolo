    FIELD MANUAL

    How to Use the App

    QUICK START
    Normal turn flowCommand → set the snapshot → activate the queued NPO → resolve the result → repeat. Use Roster whenever an enemy operative attacks an NPO.
    01

    Choose the mission and roster

    Open Mission to select a scenario and track progress. Open Roster to use the default Necrons, generate a force, or edit behavior, wounds, save, and attack profile.

    02

    Review the queued NPO

    The Command tab identifies the next NPO before activation. Its roster card also carries a NEXT marker. The selection stays locked while you complete the snapshot.

    03

    Set the NPO Perspective Snapshot

    Enter range, line of sight, charge availability, cover, objectives, and nearby targets for the displayed NPO. These choices represent what that NPO can see, reach, or prioritize, and they feed its behavior decision tree.

    04

    Activate the NPO

    Press Activate Next NPO. Follow the order, movement, target priority, action sequence, and visual attack roll shown by the app.

    05

    Read the dice

    Dice use normal pip faces. Gold is a critical hit or save, green is a normal hit or save, and dim gray is a miss.

    CriticalHit / SaveMiss
    06

    Resolve an enemy attack

    Open Roster and select Resolve Enemy Attack on the target. Enter retained hits, damage, NPO Defense Dice, NPO AP reduction, and cover. The app rolls saves and updates wounds.

    07

    Advance the battle

    Threat increases automatically when NPOs Shoot or Fight, when confirmed enemy actions trigger it, and when Operate Hatch or Breach checks succeed. Use Start Next Turning Point to advance the counter, ready survivors, generate reinforcements, check events, and record the result.

    08

    Save the session

    The browser saves automatically. Use Export Save for a JSON backup and Import Save to restore it elsewhere.

    Important tabletop notes

    • This is a decision and tracking aid. Use current datacards and official rules for visibility, cover, weapon rules, combat sequencing, and mission maps.
    • An NPO at 0 wounds is dimmed, marked with a skull, and removed from the activation queue.
    • The AI decision principles and tie-break guidance are included below in this Instructions tab.

    AI REFERENCE

    How the Enemy AI Decides

    Use these principles whenever the app presents a choice that still needs a tabletop judgement.

    01

    Queue the operative

    The next ready NPO is selected and locked before you complete the NPO Perspective Snapshot, so the context always applies to the operative shown.

    02

    Follow behavior

    Brawlers close and fight. Marksmen seek clear shots. Sentinels fight nearby threats, then bring ranged weapons to bear.

    03

    Break ties against the player

    Choose the result most likely to incapacitate an enemy operative or prevent mission success. Randomize only true ties.

    04

    Resolve on the tabletop

    The app provides order, movement, target, action sequence, and a visual attack roll. Apply defence and special rules from the datacards you are using.
